About Subhash Chandra Bose

Subhash Chandra Bose was an Indian nationalist leader who attempted to gain India's independence from British rule by force during the waning years of World War II. He is commonly regarded as one of the most influential figures in Indian history.

Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Orissa, Bose studied in Calcutta (now Kolkata) and was known for his fierce patriotism and unyielding belief in India's freedom.

He formed the Indian National Army (INA) to overthrow British rule in India with the help of Axis powers during World War II. His slogan "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" galvanized the masses.

Timeline of Subhash Chandra Bose

  • 1897: Born in Cuttack, Orissa.
  • 1919: Joined Indian National Congress.
  • 1943: Formed the Azad Hind Government in Singapore.
  • 1945: Died under mysterious circumstances in a plane crash.

Legacy of Subhash Chandra Bose

Subhash Chandra Bose's legacy remains a controversial and significant part of India's struggle for independence. His bravery and leadership inspired millions, and his contributions to the freedom movement are remembered to this day.

The INA trials and his slogans continue to evoke strong emotions among Indians, and he is remembered as "Netaji" (Respected Leader) by many.
